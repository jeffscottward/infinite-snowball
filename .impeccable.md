## Design Context

### Users
Infinite Snowball serves two overlapping groups: players who want an immediately understandable rolling-and-collecting browser game, and creators who want to discover, install, author, and share modular levels, characters, objects, campaigns, and music. Players may arrive on desktop, gamepad, or mobile touch devices and may be offline after installation. Creators need transparent state, trustworthy package errors, and a clear path from browsing to authoring.

### Brand Personality
Playful, surprising, and welcoming. The product should feel like a handmade winter toybox: tactile enough to invite play, clear enough to trust, and odd enough to remember. Humor comes from scale, collection, and cheerful visual juxtapositions rather than copied characters, story, terminology, music, logo treatment, interface, or trade dress from Katamari Damacy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the settled snow-paper identity: pale snow fields, layered cut-paper labels, toy-block controls, hand-drawn route lines, collectible stickers, restrained physical shadows, and selective bursts of saturated color. The core palette is snow `#F8FAFC`, ink `#1E293B`, action blue `#2563EB`, and orange accent `#F97316` with ink text. Use Baloo 2 for short display copy and Atkinson Hyperlegible for body and interface copy. Bundle fonts locally for offline use. The visual system is light-first, responsive, legible over a 3D scene, and original rather than a reproduction of Katamari branding.

### Design Principles
1. Make the next action obvious: Press Start, Play, Browse Packs, and Create a Pack are semantic, named, and visually ranked.
2. Let the 3D world lead: interface chrome frames gameplay and state without competing with it or creating frame-loop churn.
3. Make system state trustworthy: boot, offline, storage, install, integrity, save import/export, and recovery states use plain language, stable semantics, and no color-only signals.
4. Design for every input from the start: keyboard, standards-based gamepad, pointer, and touch receive equivalent semantic actions and visible prompts. A shared `up/down/left/right/confirm/back/pause` contract drives deterministic DOM focus without raw device reads; general targets are at least 44 px and coarse-pointer gameplay targets at least 48 px.
5. Keep the identity handmade but disciplined: expressive paper/sticker asymmetry sits on a strict 4/8 spacing rhythm, restrained shadows, consistent type, and verified WCAG 2.2 AA behavior, including 320 CSS px/400% reflow, 200% text, text spacing, forced colors, and screen-reader announcements.
6. Prefer original, cleared assets and truthful captures. Do not use copied trade dress, soundtrack material, fake ratings, fake reviews, store badges, or placeholder claims presented as real.
7. Respect comfort and performance: use 150/220/300 ms motion tiers, reduced-motion alternatives, no flashing, no autoplay high-resolution loops, and no React state updates from the render frame loop.
